Gametogenesis: Oogenesis & Spermatogenesis

Oogenesis (Female)

  • Begins in fetal life: oogonia → primary oocytes (arrested in prophase I).
  • At birth: ~1–2 million primary oocytes; by puberty ~400,000.
  • Each month: FSH & LH stimulate completion of meiosis I → secondary oocyte + polar body.
  • Secondary oocyte arrested at metaphase II; completes meiosis only if fertilization occurs.
  • Result: 1 ovum + 2–3 polar bodies (degenerate).

Spermatogenesis (Male)

  • Begins at puberty in seminiferous tubules.
  • Spermatogonia → primary spermatocytes → secondary spermatocytes → spermatids → spermatozoa.
  • Entire process: ~64–72 days.
  • Each primary spermatocyte produces 4 functional sperm.
  • Sperm maturation occurs in epididymis (gain motility & ability to fertilize).

Pillitteri Note: Oogenesis yields one ovum to conserve cytoplasmic nutrients; spermatogenesis produces millions daily. Timing of gamete release is critical for fertilization.

Ovulation & Sperm Transport

Ovulation

  • Trigger: LH surge (mid-cycle, day 14 of 28-day cycle).
  • Release of secondary oocyte from Graafian follicle.
  • Ovum viable for 12–24 hours after ovulation.
  • Fimbriae sweep oocyte into fallopian tube (ampulla) – usual site of fertilization.

Sperm Journey

  • Ejaculation: ~200–500 million sperm deposited in vagina.
  • Sperm survive 48–72 hours in female reproductive tract.
  • Capacitation: biochemical changes in female tract (removal of glycoproteins, enhanced motility).
  • Only ~200 sperm reach the ampulla.
  • Acrosome reaction: release of hyaluronidase & proteases to penetrate corona radiata and zona pellucida.
Fertile window: 5 days before ovulation + day of ovulation (sperm longevity + ovum viability).

Fertilization: The Molecular Dance

1

Penetration of corona radiata: Sperm acrosomal enzymes disperse follicular cells.

2

Zona pellucida binding & acrosome reaction: Sperm binds to ZP3 receptors → acrosome releases enzymes (acrosin).

3

Fusion of sperm & oocyte membranes → sperm nucleus enters cytoplasm.

4

Cortical reaction: Cortical granules release contents → zona pellucida hardens → prevents polyspermy (irreversible block).

5

Completion of meiosis II in oocyte → female pronucleus (23,X).

6

Male pronucleus forms (23,X or Y), pronuclei fuse → syngamy (zygote formation) 46 chromosomes.

Timing: Fertilization occurs within 12–24 hours post-ovulation in the ampulla of the tube.

Cleavage, Blastocyst & Implantation

  • Cleavage: Rapid mitotic divisions (first at ~30h). Zygote → 2-cell → 4-cell → morula (16 cells).
  • Morula enters uterus day 3-4.
  • Blastocyst formation: Cavitation → inner cell mass (embryoblast) and outer trophoblast.
  • Implantation window: Days 6-10 after fertilization.
  • Trophoblast secretes hCG (maintains corpus luteum → progesterone).
  • Decidual reaction: endometrium becomes secretory, vascularized.
Ectopic pregnancy: implantation outside uterine cavity (most often ampulla of tube).

Nursing Implications (Preconception & Early Pregnancy)

Folic acid supplementation (400-800 mcg daily) before conception & first trimester reduces neural tube defects.
Fertility awareness: Teach women about fertile window (cervical mucus changes, BBT).
Preconception counseling: Avoid teratogens (alcohol, smoking), manage chronic conditions (diabetes, hypothyroidism).
Early diagnosis of pregnancy: Urine/serum hCG detection (as early as 8-10 days post-fertilization).
Signs of possible implantation: light spotting (implantation bleed) ~6-12 days after conception; educate about normal vs abnormal bleeding.
Genetic & carrier screening for couples with family history.
